Pediatric Asthma in ER

MILD SEVERITY MANAGEMENT

  • Inhaled SABA by nebulizer (or MDI with valved holding chamber) ± ipratropium bromide ± Oxygen, up to 3 doses in first hour.
    • SABA is superior to ipratropium (see notes)
  • Oral systemic corticosteroids if no immediate response or if the patient recently took oral systemic corticosteroids.
  • Reassess after each nebulizer.
  • Continue treatment 1-3 hours, provided there is improvement.
  • Make admission decision in less than 4 hours
  • If there is good response:
    • Discharge home.
    • Continue treatment with inhaled SABA.
    • Continue course of oral systemic corticosteroid.
    • Consider initiation of an ICS.
  • Patient education:
    • Review medications, including inhaler technique.
    • Review predisposing factors.
    • Recommend follow up with pediatrician within 1 week.

MODERATE SEVERITY MANAGEMENT

  • Start with the following medications:
    • Salbutamol (neb) every 2hr ± Oxygen.
    • Steroids (IV or PO).
    • Hydration.
  • Assess after 4 hours.
    • If the child is not improving follow the severe management.
    • If the child is improving:
      • Decrease frequency of nebulizers gradually by increasing 1 hour each time to ≥4 hourly.
      • Assess each 6 hour.
        • If the child is not improving:
          • Continue on present treatment.
          • Add Ipratropium (nebulizer).
          • Search for predisposing cause.
          • Consider other diagnosis.
        • If the child is improving:
          • Apply discharge criteria, if fulfilled, discharge patient.

SEVERE SEVERITY MANAGEMENT

  • Start with the following medications:
    • Oxygen.
    • Salbutamol (nebulizer) hourly.
    • Steroids (IV/PO).
    • Ipratropium (nebulizer).
    • Hydration.
  • Monitor SpO2.
  • Chest X-ray.
  • Assess after 3 hrs.
    • If the child is not improving
      • consider advanced management like magnesium sulphate
      • consider communicating with pediatric pulmonologist and transferring the patient
    • If the child is improving:
      • Decrease frequency of nebulizers gradually by increasing 1 hour each time to ≥2hourly.
      • Assess each 3 hours.
      • If the child continues to improve manage as moderate severity.

Pediatric Hypoglycemia in ER

GLUCOSE THERAPY

  • Conscious cooperative child
    • 15 g (or 0.2 g/kg for infants) of rapid-acting carbohydrate should be given by mouth (can be from dextrose solutions)
    • Can be supplied by 125 ml juice or biscuits
    • IV access recommended since child's glucose may not to respond to oral intervention.
  • Patient with altered consciousness
    • IV Dextrose
    • Initial bolus 2 mL/kg of dextrose 10%
    • If glucose fails to increase after 15-20 minutes, repeat the bolus
    • Higher concentrations of dextrose are not recommended
  • Dextrose infusion
    • After the initial bolus, a dextrose infusion should be started to prevent recurrent hypoglycemia.
    • Infants should be started on a glucose infusion rate (GIR) of 5-6 mg/kg/minute (Dextrose 10%)
    • Older children 2-3 mg/kg/minute (dextrose 5%)
    • GIR should be increased by 0.5-1 mg/kg/minute if the patient continues to have plasma glucoses <70 mg/dL.
    • GIR = (dextrose percentage rate of infusion [mL/hr]) (6 weight [kg])
  • Glucagon
    • If the child's mental status is altered and intravenous access cannot be obtained,
    • 1 mg intramuscularly (regardless of body weight).
    • Glucagon is only effective for patients with suspected insulin-mediated hypoglycemia (hyperinsulinism, insulin administration, or sulfonylurea ingestion Glucagon is not effective in other forms of hypoglycemia)

MONITORING

  • Every 15-20 minutes until it is >70 mg/dL
  • Thereafter, it can be checked hourly to ensure stability, and then subsequent checks can be further spaced to every three to four hours.

Pediatric Bell's Palsy

Drug therapy for Bell's palsy

  • Steroids
    • Begin within three days of symptom onset
    • Prednisone 1-2 mg/kg daily (up to 60 to 80 mg) for five days, followed by a five-day taper. (1 mg/kg may be similarly effective).
  • Acyclovir
    • Used for
      • For herpes evidence
      • Severe Bell's palsy
    • 5 days
    • PO: 1000–1200 mg/24 hr ÷ 3–5 doses per 24 hr × 5 days. For pediatric dosing, use 40–80 mg/kg/24 hr ÷ Q6–8 hr × 5–10 days (max. pediatric dose: 1000 mg/24 hr)

Eye care

  • Appropriate eye care is required to help avoid corneal abrasions if the patient with facial palsy is unable to close the eye.
  • Artificial tears during the day and ophthalmic ointment and patching at night
  • Tape should not be placed directly on the eyelid because the patch could slip and abrade the cornea.
